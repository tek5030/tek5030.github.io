1 # Step 1: Get an overview
2 Today we will implement and experiment with a simple segmentation method based on color. You can choose to work on
3 images from your camera or on the provided videos captured from an autonomous vehicle.
4 
5 The idea is that each pixel corresponds to a 3-dimensional feature vector [R,G,B] (or [B,G,R] in OpenCV).
6 Pixels with similar colors should be in the same region of the 3-dimensional feature space.
7 If we know what kind of pixels we are looking for, we can represent them using a multivariate normal distribution.
8 We can then use the Mahalanobis distance to determine how well a pixel fit with the distribution.
9 Doing so for all pixels in an image gives us an image of Mahalanobis distances between the pixels and the distribution.
10 
11 By thresholding the Mahalanobis distance image we get a binary image indicating the pixels that are closer than
12 the chosen threshold.
13 
14 As we will experience, this simplistic approach is far from perfect. But the method can naturally be generalized and
15 improved:
16 
17 - Each pixel can be represented by more features than just color, thus increasing the dimension of the feature space.
18 - The group of pixels that we want to segment out might not fit well with a multivariate normal distribution, so we can
19  use a more flexible distribution instead.

22 ## Lab overview
23 
24 The main steps in today's lab are:
25 
26 - **Estimate a multivariate normal distribution** based on the pixels in a predefined sample region of a single image.
27 
28  ![illustration of the estimation process](img/from_rgb_to_normal_dist.png)
29 
30 - For the following images, **create a Mahalanobis image** by computing the Mahalanobis distance between each pixel
31 and the multivariate normal distribution. Transform the Mahalanobis image to get an image in the [0,1] range.
32 
33 - **Create a binary image** by thresholding the transformed Mahalanobis image manually or by using Otsu's method.
34 The final binary image is the result of our segmentation method and we can use it to highlight the corresponding pixels
35 in the original image. If the result is noisy, we can clean up this binary image using morphological operations.
36 
37  ![Illustration of the evaluation process](img/from_rgb_and_normal_dist_to_segmentation.png)
38 
39  - We will also implement a way to gradually update the multivariate normal distribution based on how the pixels in the
40  sampling region change over time.
41 
42  Finally we will use our segmentation method to perform road segmentation in videos captured from an autonomous
43  vehicle.
